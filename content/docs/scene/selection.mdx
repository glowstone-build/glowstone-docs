## How selection works

Glowstone tracks selection by kind. At any moment one *active kind* is selected — **Fixtures**, **Objects** (static geometry), **Screens** (LED walls), or **Pyro** ([devices](/docs/fixtures/pyro)) — and you can multi-select within that kind. The **World** and an **Environment** are single-only selections.

<Callout type="info" title="One kind at a time">
The four multi-select kinds are mutually exclusive. Selecting a fixture clears any object, screen or pyro selection, and vice versa. You cannot have fixtures and screens selected together. When nothing is selected, the active kind defaults to **Fixtures**.
</Callout>

### The primary selection

The first object in the active kind is the **primary** selection. It drives single-edit UI and acts as the reference for the Quick-Select palette (for example, "same type" and "same maker" match the primary fixture's profile and manufacturer). When you build a multi-selection, the primary row is highlighted more strongly — a brighter outline plus an accent bar down its left edge.

## Click and modifiers

Every click maps to a clear rule. The rules apply whether you click in the 3D Viewport or on a row in the [Scene Outliner](/docs/scene/outliner), with one difference noted below.

| Click | Result |
| --- | --- |
| `Click` | Replace — the selection becomes the object you hit. Clicking empty space clears everything. |
| `Cmd`/`Ctrl`+`Click` | Toggle — flip membership of the single hit. Clicking an off-kind object switches to that kind. |
| `Shift`+`Click` (viewport) | Add — union the hit into the current selection (within the active kind). |
| `Shift`+`Click` (outliner, fixtures) | Extend a range from the anchor to the clicked fixture (see below). |

For marquee (box) selection, see [Box-select](#box-select) with `B` below.

<Callout type="info" title="Cmd vs Ctrl">
On macOS use `Cmd`; on Windows and Linux use `Ctrl`. The two are interchangeable throughout Glowstone.
</Callout>

### Replace, add, toggle, subtract

<Steps>

<Step>
### Replace
A plain `Click` discards the old selection and selects only what you hit. Click empty space to deselect everything.
</Step>

<Step>
### Add to the selection
In the viewport, hold `Shift` and click to union more objects into the selection. Add stays within the active kind — `Shift`+clicking a screen while fixtures are selected has no effect on the fixtures. (In the outliner, `Shift`+click on a fixture extends a range instead — see below.)
</Step>

<Step>
### Toggle a single object
Hold `Cmd`/`Ctrl` and click to flip one object in or out of the selection. Toggling an object of a *different* kind switches the active kind to that object.
</Step>

<Step>
### Box-select
Press `B` to arm box-select, then drag a marquee in the viewport. It is **additive** — it adds to the current selection and **auto-exits** after each box, so press `B` again to box more; hold `Cmd`/`Ctrl` on release to subtract instead. While armed, a crosshair tracks the cursor and a cue shows the active filter: press `F` for **fixtures only** or `O` for **objects only** (default is everything except fog volumes). `Esc` or a right-click cancels before you drag.
</Step>

</Steps>

### Range selection (outliner, fixtures)

In the [Scene Outliner](/docs/scene/outliner), range selection extends an inclusive run of fixtures, useful for grabbing a contiguous block in a long rig.

- A plain click or a toggle click sets the **anchor**.
- `Shift`+click a second fixture to select every fixture between the anchor and the click, inclusive.
- The range runs over the **visible, sorted order** — whatever search filter and sort order are currently applied in the [Scene Outliner](/docs/scene/outliner). Reordering or filtering changes which fixtures fall inside the range.
- The anchor drops if its fixture is deleted.

<Callout type="idea">
Sort the outliner by patch address, click the first fixture, then `Shift`+click the last — you have selected a whole consecutive patch block in two clicks.
</Callout>

## Select all, invert and deselect

These commands act on the active kind. They are part of the [global keymap](/docs/reference/keyboard-shortcuts).

| Shortcut | Command | Result |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `A` | Select all | Select every object of the active kind |
| `Cmd`/`Ctrl`+`I` | Invert selection | Flip membership within the active kind |
| `Alt`+`A` | Deselect all | Clear the selection |
| `Esc` | Deselect all | Clear the selection |

<Callout type="warn" title="Viewport focus masks S">
When the 3D Viewport is focused, `S` is captured by the Scale transform, so `S` opens the Quick-Select palette only when the viewport is *not* focused. (`Shift`+`A` opens the viewport's Add menu.) The selection shortcuts above stay live in the viewport — plain `A` still selects all. See [Commands & Operator Search](/docs/interface/commands).
</Callout>

## The Quick-Select palette

Press `S` (with the viewport not focused) to open the Quick-Select palette — a fast batch-selection menu that operates on the **active kind**. With fixtures active it grabs families of fixtures based on the primary; with objects, screens or pyro active it still offers All / Invert / None. When nothing is selected the palette defaults to fixtures. Each row shows a count of how many items it would select, plus its key badge.

| Key | Option | Selects |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `A` | `All <kind>` | Every item of the active kind |
| `T` | All of this type | Every fixture sharing the primary's profile |
| `M` | All by this maker | Every fixture sharing the primary's GDTF manufacturer |
| `I` | Invert selection | Flips membership within the active kind |
| `N` | Select none | Clears the selection |

- The `A` row's label follows the active kind — **All fixtures**, **All objects**, **All screens** or **All pyro**.
- **All of this type** (`T`) and **All by this maker** (`M`) are **fixture-only**. With objects, screens or pyro active they render disabled (the rows stay in the layout so the key mapping is stable).
- **All of this type** (`T`) is enabled only when the active kind is fixtures and the primary has a profile.
- **All by this maker** (`M`) is enabled only when the primary is a GDTF-backed fixture with a manufacturer. Built-in profiles (PAR Can, lasers) have no maker.
- **All**, **Invert** and **Select none** are always available.
- `Esc` dismisses the palette without changing the selection.

<Accordions type="single">
<Accordion title="Select every fixture from one manufacturer">
Select one fixture of that brand (for example an ETC Source Four), press `S`, then press `M`. Every ETC fixture in the scene is now selected. Press `Esc` or click away to close the palette.
</Accordion>
<Accordion title="Why are T and M greyed out?">
Both are fixture-only and depend on the primary selection. `T` needs a fixture primary with a profile; `M` needs a GDTF-backed primary that carries a manufacturer name. With nothing selected, with a non-fixture kind active, or with a built-in profile that has no maker, these rows are disabled.
</Accordion>
</Accordions>

The same actions are also reachable from the [operator search](/docs/interface/commands). For the full list of selection commands and their IDs, see the [Command Reference](/docs/reference/commands).

## Named selection groups

Glowstone stores named selection groups — console-style saved collections of **fixtures**. Groups are part of the document: they are saved with the project ([`.glow`](/docs/projects)) and captured in undo snapshots alongside the scene, DMX patch and cues, so building or changing a group is undoable. Their fixture references are remapped when you delete or reorder fixtures, so a stored group stays valid as you edit the rig.

<Callout type="info" title="Fixtures only">
Selection groups hold fixtures only — not objects, screens or pyro devices.
</Callout>
