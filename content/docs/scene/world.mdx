The **World** node holds a single equirectangular HDRI that does two jobs at once: it paints the sky background behind your set and lights every surface with image-based ambient fill. There is one World per scene, and it lives at the top of the [Scene Outliner](/docs/scene/outliner).

## What the World does

<Cards>
  <Card icon={<Image />} title="Sky background">
    With `show_background` on, the HDRI is drawn behind the scene as the visible sky.
  </Card>
  <Card icon={<Sun />} title="Ambient lighting">
    The same map provides image-based fill on geometry, so surfaces are never pure black.
  </Card>
</Cards>

The World is the only source of ambient image-based light. It complements your fixtures with a backdrop and soft fill, rather than replacing them.

## Selecting the World

Click the **World** row at the top of the Scene Outliner to select it. The World is a single-only selection: choosing it clears any other selection (fixtures, objects, screens, pyro or environment), and selecting any of those clears the World.

<Callout type="info">
With the World selected, the Inspector also shows the **Render Properties** panel. The World's `Brightness`, `Ambient`, `Rotation` and `Background` controls are mirrored there in the **World** section alongside the rest of the render look. See [Rendering Overview](/docs/rendering) for the full panel.
</Callout>

## Loading an HDRI

<Steps>
<Step>
### Select the World
Click the **World** node in the Scene Outliner.
</Step>
<Step>
### Load the map
Choose an equirectangular HDRI file. Supported formats are `.hdr` (Radiance HDR), `.exr` (OpenEXR), `.png`, and `.jpg`.
</Step>
<Step>
### Shape the look
Adjust `brightness` and `ambient` for overall level, and `rotation` to spin the sky and its lighting to the angle you want.
</Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="idea">
Use a true HDR format (`.hdr` or `.exr`) when you want a bright sun or sky that still reads after exposure and bloom. LDR `.png`/`.jpg` maps work for a flat backdrop but carry less highlight energy.
</Callout>

## Parameters

<TypeTable
  type={{
    brightness: {
      description: 'Overall multiplier on the environment (sky background and ambient contribution together).',
      type: '0.0–4.0',
      default: '1.0',
    },
    ambient: {
      description: 'How strongly the map illuminates geometry as image-based diffuse fill.',
      type: '0.0–2.0',
      default: '1.0',
    },
    rotation: {
      description: 'Azimuth rotation of the environment about the vertical (Y) axis. Turns the sky and its lighting together.',
      type: '0–2π rad',
      default: '0',
    },
    show_background: {
      description: 'Draw the map as the visible sky. Off uses the HDRI for lighting only, leaving a dark void behind the set.',
      type: 'on / off',
      default: 'on',
    },
  }}
/>

<Callout type="info" title="Lighting without a sky">
Turn `show_background` off when you want the ambient fill from the HDRI but a clean dark background — useful for isolated render plates while keeping natural fill on the set.
</Callout>

## How the HDRI is loaded

The map is decoded on the CPU and uploaded as a 16-bit float texture (`Rgba16Float`) so it carries bright highlights without clipping. A few limits shape what to feed it:

<TypeTable
  type={{
    'Max width': {
      description: 'Maps are capped at 1024 px wide. Wider maps are resampled down with the source aspect ratio preserved.',
      type: 'pixels',
      default: '1024',
    },
    'Mip chain': {
      description: 'Box-filtered mips are built from full resolution down, giving a sharp sky and softer blurred fill for ambient lighting.',
      type: 'CPU mips',
      default: '—',
    },
    Wrapping: {
      description: 'The horizontal (azimuth) axis wraps so rotation is seamless; the vertical axis clamps at the poles. Trilinear mip filtering.',
      type: 'U wrap / V clamp',
      default: '—',
    },
  }}
/>

<Callout type="warn">
There is no benefit to importing maps wider than `1024` px — anything larger is downsampled on load. Resize wide panoramas to roughly that width before importing to avoid wasted decode time.
</Callout>

<Accordions type="single">
  <Accordion title="Why is the background dark even though I loaded an HDRI?">
    Check that `show_background` is on. With it off, the map still lights the scene but is not drawn as the sky, leaving a dark void. Also confirm `brightness` is above 0.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What happens before any HDRI is loaded?">
    With no map loaded, the World uses a flat placeholder and provides no image-based fill. Load a map to get both sky and ambient lighting.
  </Accordion>

</Accordions>
