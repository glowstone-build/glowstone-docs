From cold launch to a volumetric beam in haze in a few minutes, using Glowstone's default demo scene plus one fixture you add yourself.

<Callout type="info" title="What you already have">
Every new project starts with a small demo scene: one **PAR Can 1** at full intensity, hung 4 m up and aimed 45° down-and-forward, inside one **Fog Box** haze volume, over the world floor. That is enough to see beams immediately, before you add anything.
</Callout>

## Build your first lit scene

<Steps>

<Step>
### Launch and start a new project

Open Glowstone. The **Welcome** splash appears with a `glowstone` wordmark and two columns — **New** on the left, **Recent Files** on the right.

- Click **New Project** (under **New**) to create a blank scene.
- Or click **Continue without a project** in the footer to keep the demo scene already loaded.

You can reopen this splash any time from **Window → Welcome**, and create a fresh project from **File → New Project**.

<Callout type="idea">
Right after launch you already have a lit beam in haze. If you only want to look around, skip straight to step 2.
</Callout>
</Step>

<Step>
### Orbit, pan, and zoom the viewport

Navigate the 3D stage with the mouse inside the **Viewport**:

| Gesture | Action |
| --- | --- |
| `Drag` | Orbit the camera around the target |
| `Shift`+`Drag` | Pan the camera |
| `Scroll` | Zoom in and out |

Press `F` to frame the current selection, or `Shift`+`F` to frame the whole scene. Jump to a preset angle from **View → Camera** (Top, Bottom, Front, Back, Left, Right).

<Callout type="info" title="Save a view for later">
**Window → View Bookmarks → Save Current View** stores the camera pose to the next free numbered slot (1–9). These camera bookmarks persist and are separate from cues. See [Camera Bookmarks](/docs/navigation/bookmarks).
</Callout>
</Step>

<Step>
### Add a fixture

You have two ways to put a new light in the scene.

<Tabs items={['Add menu (Shift+A)', 'From the Library']}>
  <Tab value="Add menu (Shift+A)">
Press `Shift`+`A` in the Viewport to open the cursor-anchored **Add** menu. Its left column lists the categories — **Fixtures**, **Screens**, **Pyro**, **Environment** — and the right column lists that category's entries. Pick a fixture (for example **PAR Can**); it is created on the ground at the cursor location.
  </Tab>
  <Tab value="From the Library">
Switch to the **Library** tab in the left panel, browse the built-in fixtures, and place a profile such as **PAR Can**. You can also **Import GDTF…** to load a real moving head. See [The Fixture Library](/docs/library).
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

New fixtures are placed aimed down-and-forward (`tilt` 30°) so they light the floor right away.

<ImagePlaceholder
  label="Close-up"
  ratio="4 / 3"
  caption="The Shift+A Add menu open over the 3D Viewport at the cursor position, the left column showing Fixtures / Screens / Pyro / Environment, with a PAR Can entry highlighted on the right."
/>

<Callout type="info">
The **Pyro** category adds CO2 jets and cold-spark machines — placed devices with their own inline DMX patch, separate from the fixture patch. See [Pyro Devices](/docs/fixtures/pyro).
</Callout>
</Step>

<Step>
### Select it and aim it

Click the fixture in the Viewport to select it (or click its row in the **Scene** outliner). The header in the **Inspector** on the right now shows that fixture.

Aim the beam in either of two ways:

- **Pan / Tilt fields:** type or drag `Pan` and `Tilt` (in degrees) in the Inspector's **Fixture** group. `Tilt` 0 aims straight down `-Y`; 45 aims down-and-forward.
- **Aim tool:** drag to point the selected head at the cursor; Glowstone solves the `Pan` and `Tilt` for you. See [Pan, Tilt & Aiming](/docs/fixtures/movement).

<Callout type="info">
Use `Cmd`/`Ctrl`+click to toggle fixtures in a multi-selection, and `Shift`+click in the outliner to select a range. See [Selecting Objects](/docs/scene/selection).
</Callout>
</Step>

<Step>
### Raise the dimmer and light the haze

In the Inspector's **Fixture** group, set the fixture's **Dimmer** (`0`–`1`) up toward `1.0`. The beam brightens and a volumetric shaft appears where it crosses the **Fog Box**.

A few parameters shape what you see (Beam controls live under the group's **Advanced** disclosure):

<TypeTable
  type={{
    Dimmer: { description: 'Master dimmer for the fixture.', type: '0–1', default: 'profile default' },
    Beam: { description: 'Volumetric beam intensity multiplier (0 = off, 1 = normal). Scales the visible shaft and floor pool without touching the lens face.', type: '0–4', default: '1.0' },
    Color: { description: 'Emitted beam colour (linear RGB).', type: 'RGB', default: 'profile default' },
    'Beam angle': { description: 'Full cone angle (built-in fixtures only; GDTF heads drive it from Zoom).', type: '2–90°', default: 'profile default' },
  }}
/>

If the shaft looks thin or invisible, the haze may be too light. Select the **Fog Box** in the outliner and raise its `Density` (default `0.03` is a light haze with visible beams; range `0`–`4`). See [Environments: Fog & Haze](/docs/scene/environment) and [Volumetric Beams & Haze](/docs/rendering/volumetrics).

<ImagePlaceholder
  label="Screenshot"

  caption="A single fixture at full intensity casting a sharp volumetric beam shaft down through the Fog Box onto the floor, with the Inspector on the right showing the Dimmer near maximum."
/>

<Callout type="warn">
The volumetric beam uses per-pixel raymarching by default, which looks cleaner. The optional froxel volumetric grid is off by default and is not the recommended path.
</Callout>
</Step>

<Step>
### (Optional) Drive it from a console or record a cue

Your fixture is lit and aimed. Two natural next moves:

<Tabs items={['Patch & feed from a console', 'Record a cue']}>
  <Tab value="Patch & feed from a console">
Glowstone **receives** live DMX from a console over Art-Net or sACN; it never transmits DMX.

1. Select the fixture and press `P` to open the **Patch** dialog. Set a starting **Universe** and **Address**, then confirm — selected fixtures are packed sequentially from there.
2. Open the **Connectivity** tab. Art-Net and sACN are both enabled by default and the receiver starts automatically; leave the **Bind interface** at `0.0.0.0` and confirm the console appears in **Sources**.
3. Fade the fixture on the console; the 3D fixture follows in real time.

See [Patching Fixtures](/docs/dmx/patching) and [Connecting a Console](/docs/dmx/consoles).
  </Tab>
  <Tab value="Record a cue">
Open the **Cues** tab and store the current state of all fixtures as a named cue. Recall cues later with a crossfade. See [Cues & Playback](/docs/cues).
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Callout type="warn">
Glowstone is input-only for DMX. It visualises what a console sends but never outputs or transmits DMX itself.
</Callout>
</Step>

</Steps>

## Frequently asked questions

<Accordions type="single">
  <Accordion title="My beam is invisible. What's wrong?">
    Check three things: the fixture's `Dimmer` is above `0`, the fixture's `Beam` multiplier is above `0`, and the beam actually passes through the **Fog Box**. Volumetric shafts only render inside the haze volume, so a beam aimed outside the box shows just a floor pool.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Where did the demo scene come from?">
    Every new project seeds a demo scene (one PAR Can, one Fog Box, over the world floor) so the beam engine and volumetrics are visible without importing anything. Delete or edit these freely.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Does my work save automatically?">
    Save with `Cmd`/`Ctrl`+`S` to write a `.glow` project file. Glowstone also keeps a crash-recovery autosave, offered as **Recover Last Session** on the Welcome splash. See [Projects & the .glow Format](/docs/projects).
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="The fixture won't react to my console.">
    Confirm the fixture is patched (`enabled`) at the universe and address the console is driving, that the matching protocol is enabled in **Connectivity**, and that the console appears in **Sources**. See [The DMX Monitor](/docs/dmx/dmx-monitor) to inspect live channel values.
  </Accordion>
</Accordions>
