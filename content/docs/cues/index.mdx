A cue is a snapshot of your rig's look — every fixture's pan, tilt, colour, intensity, beam and full optical chain captured in one click. Recall a cue and Glowstone crossfades back to that look over a fade time you set. Built for shaping and showing looks offline, without a live console.

## What a cue stores

Recording a cue captures one look per fixture, aligned to the fixtures in the scene at record time. Each look holds the fixture's full controllable state.

<Callout type="info" title="How recall fades">
Continuous fields — pan, tilt, colour, intensity, beam, dimmer, zoom, focus, iris, CTO, green and CMY — crossfade smoothly over the cue's fade time. Discrete optics — gobo, colour, animation, prism and frost wheel slots — **snap instantly** at the start of the fade, because wheel slots don't interpolate. Pan and tilt take the shortest path (e.g. `+170°` to `-170°` crosses `0°`, not the long way round).
</Callout>

## The Cues panel

Open the **Cues** tab in the dockable sidebar. The panel has three parts: a record section, a transport, and the scrollable cue list.

### Cue list columns

The cue list is a scrollable grid showing every recorded cue in order. The current cue (the one last fully recalled) is highlighted in the panel's accent colour.

| Column | Content | Interaction |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `N. Name` | Cue index and label, e.g. `1. Cyc Wash` | Left-click to recall the cue |
| `X fx` | Fixture count captured in the cue | Read-only |
| Fade slider | Per-cue crossfade time, range `0.0`–`60.0 s` | Drag to edit (suffix `s`) |
| Trash icon | Delete | Left-click removes the cue |

<Callout type="idea">
The `X fx` column is handy for verifying a cue's content — if a cue shows fewer fixtures than the rig, it was recorded before you added the rest.
</Callout>

### Record section

- **Name field** — type a label such as `Intro WL`. Leave it blank and the cue auto-numbers (the placeholder shows the next number, e.g. `Cue 5`).
- **Fade-time control** — drag to set the new cue's crossfade duration. Default `2.0 s`, range `0.0`–`60.0 s`.
- **Record button** — captures the current look of every fixture. Disabled when the scene has no fixtures.

### Transport

- **Prev** — steps to and recalls the previous cue. Only works when a cue is already active; it does not jump to the last cue from a cold start.
- **Go** — the strong, accent-coloured button. Fires the next cue in the list (or cue 1 if none is active) and stops at the last cue.
- **Current cue label** — shows a play icon and the active cue name, e.g. `▶ Cyc Wash`, or `—` when none is active.
- **Fade progress bar** — appears only during an active fade, showing progress from `0` to `1.0` with the target cue name, e.g. `→ Spot A`.

When the list is empty the panel reads `none — set a look (Inspector / DMX), then Record`.

## Record a cue

<Steps>
<Step>
### Set the look
Position, colour and control every fixture using the [Inspector](/docs/interface#inspector) or [live DMX input](/docs/dmx/connectivity) until the rig shows the look you want.
</Step>
<Step>
### Name it (optional)
Type a label in the Cues panel name field, or leave it blank to auto-number.
</Step>
<Step>
### Set the fade time (optional)
Drag the record-section fade control. This is the crossfade duration used when you later recall this cue. Default `2.0 s`.
</Step>
<Step>
### Click Record
Glowstone captures the position, colour, intensity and optics of every fixture into a new cue at the bottom of the list. The name field clears, ready for the next cue.
</Step>
</Steps>

## Play back a cue

<Tabs items={['Click the list', 'Go / Prev']}>
  <Tab value="Click the list">
Left-click a cue's name in the list to recall it immediately. Discrete optics snap, then the continuous fields crossfade from the current state to the cue's look over its fade time.
  </Tab>
  <Tab value="Go / Prev">
Use **Go** to step forward through the list one cue at a time and **Prev** to step back. Both recall the cue they land on using that cue's fade time. Go stops at the last cue; Prev only works once a cue is active.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Callout type="success">
Set the fade time to `0 s` for an instant snap with no crossfade — useful for blackout or bump cues.
</Callout>

## Cues and a live console

<Callout type="warn" title="Don't run cues and a console at once">
The cue engine runs after live-DMX decode each frame. While a fade is in progress, **the cue output wins over live DMX**; outside a fade, live DMX drives the rig normally. See [Connecting a Console](/docs/dmx/connectivity).
</Callout>

## Persistence and undo

<Callout type="info">
Cues are saved in the `.glow` [project file](/docs/projects) alongside the scene, patch, camera and render settings. Cue state is part of the document the undo system snapshots. An in-progress fade is transient: it is not saved, and on reload the fade is cleared while the current cue index is restored.
</Callout>

## Cues vs. camera bookmarks

A cue stores a **fixture look**. A [camera bookmark](/docs/navigation/bookmarks) stores a **camera pose** — where you are looking from in the 3D scene. They are separate systems: bookmarks never touch fixtures, and cues never move the camera. Use bookmarks (9 numbered slots) to jump between viewpoints, and cues to switch between rig states.

## Limitations

<Callout type="warn">
The cue list does not yet support renaming or reordering. A cue's name is set at record time only, and cues stay in the order they were recorded — to change the order, delete and re-record. Cue groups and submasters are not implemented.
</Callout>

## Frequently asked questions

<Accordions type="single">
  <Accordion title="What happens to my cues if I delete a fixture?">
    Every cue's look list drops that fixture's entry, the current cue index is adjusted if needed, and any in-progress fade is aborted because its snapshot no longer aligns with the rig.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I change a cue's fade time after recording?">
    Yes. Drag the fade slider in the cue list's third column. The change is per-cue and does not require re-recording.
  </Accordion>

</Accordions>
