## Pan and tilt

Each fixture stores two angle pairs. The **commanded** angles are the targets you (or DMX) ask for; the **actual** angles are where the head currently points as it slews.

- **Pan** is yaw — rotation about the world Y (up) axis, in degrees.
- **Tilt** is pitch — rotation about the head's local X axis, in degrees. `0` points straight **down**; positive tilt swings the beam down-forward (`45` is down-and-forward).

The beam exits along the head's local `-Y`, from the lens `0.32 m` below the head body.

<Callout type="info" title="Setting angles">
Edit `pan` and `tilt` directly in the Inspector, or let a patched console write them over DMX. Glowstone visualizes incoming DMX — it never transmits DMX. See [Patching Fixtures](/docs/dmx/patching) and [Connecting a Console](/docs/dmx/consoles).
</Callout>

<Callout type="info" title="Newly placed fixtures">
Fixtures dropped into the scene start tilted down (`tilt = 30°`) and snap straight to their commanded pose on placement, so they look aimed immediately rather than slewing from zero.
</Callout>

## Physically-slewed motion

Pan and tilt do not jump to their targets. Glowstone runs a **trapezoidal velocity profile**: the head ramps up to a cruise speed, holds it, then decelerates onto the target without overshoot. The rendered pose (`pan_actual` / `tilt_actual`) lags the command, exactly like a real motor.

This is why a snap cue still takes a beat to land, and why fast chases show the heads streaking through intermediate angles.

### Move speed

The `move_speed` control scales how aggressively the motors drive:

- `0` — fastest. The head effectively **tracks** the command with near-zero lag.
- `1` — slowest. Full travel takes around `25.5 s`.

<Callout type="idea">
Dial `move_speed` up for slow, cinematic fan-outs and lazy position fades; keep it at `0` when you want hard, snappy position cues that hit on the beat.
</Callout>

### Maximum slew rates

At full motor speed (`move_speed = 0`), the top slew rate depends on the fixture class. Lighter heads turn faster.

| Class | Max pan | Max tilt |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Beam | `260°/s` | `200°/s` |
| Spot (default) | `220°/s` | `180°/s` |
| Wash | `160°/s` | `130°/s` |
| Slowest motor (`move_speed = 1`) | `~21°/s` | `~11°/s` |

<Callout type="info">
At the slowest motor setting a head needs roughly `25.5 s` for full travel, so plan long position moves with that headroom in mind.
</Callout>

## The Aim tool

Rather than dialling pan and tilt by hand, point a head at a place in the world and let Glowstone solve the angles for you.

<Steps>
<Step>
### Select the head(s)
Click a moving head in the viewport or the [Scene Outliner](/docs/scene/outliner). Shift-click or box-select to aim several heads at once. See [Selecting Objects](/docs/scene/selection).
</Step>
<Step>
### Activate the Aim tool
Pick the **Aim** tool, then drag in the viewport. The selected head(s) point toward the cursor / the world point under it.
</Step>
<Step>
### Release to commit
Glowstone solves the commanded `pan` and `tilt` so each beam passes through the target, then the heads slew there at their motor speed.
</Step>
</Steps>

The solver unwraps pan the **short way** — it rotates to the nearest equivalent angle instead of spinning the long way around. If the target lands exactly on the head (no defined direction), the aim is left unchanged.

<Callout type="info" title="Convergence">
Select several heads before aiming and they all point at the same world point — the quickest way to build a tight convergence look without touching individual angles.
</Callout>

## Per-emitter pan and tilt

Some GDTF fixtures are **independent-head** designs with more than one articulated emitter. Glowstone stores per-emitter angles so each head on the body can point in its own direction:

- `cell_pan` — per-emitter pan, one entry per emitter.
- `cell_tilt` — per-emitter tilt.

The emitter layout comes from the active GDTF [DMX mode](/docs/library/dmx-modes); switching modes resizes the per-emitter arrays to match.

<Accordions type="single">
  <Accordion title="Why does my multi-head fixture only have one pan/tilt field?">
    Single-body moving heads expose one `pan` and one `tilt`. Per-emitter `cell_pan` / `cell_tilt` only apply to GDTF fixtures whose active mode defines multiple independently-articulated heads. If the Inspector shows a single pair, the active mode has one moving emitter.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Are per-emitter angles saved with the project?">
    No. `cell_pan` and `cell_tilt` are runtime-only state, rebuilt each session from the DMX mode and live input rather than stored in the `.glow` file.
  </Accordion>
</Accordions>
